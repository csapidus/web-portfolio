1453 Battle NetLogo Simulation

This NetLogo simulation of the 1453 Battle of Constantinople was written for a Military Science 4002 class project. This is the first NetLogo simulation I wrote from scratch. NetLogo boils down to two concepts - turtles and patches. In this case, the turtles (objects) took the form of Ottoman and Byzantine soldiers, and patches (locations) took the form of the city of Byzantine and corresponding seas. Details including the three Ottoman waves were kept true to recorded history. The Bosphorous Chain, which the Byzantines used to block out Ottoman ships from the Golden Horn, is included so as to better represent the sequence of occuring events. Ultiamtely, the Ottomans take over Constantinople nearly every time, though the means to doing so change each run; it is a simulation after all! This simulation is modeled in 2-D, but may be presented in 3-D if desired.
