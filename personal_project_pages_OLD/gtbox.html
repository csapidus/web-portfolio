Georgia Tech "Chaos" Treasure Box

With graduation from Georgia Tech on the horizon (spring 2018), I wanted to create at least one Georgia Tech themed project. Though the original plan was for a digital nixie clock in a wooden box, I elected to scrap the clock and just focus on the exterior. I wanted to capture the most recognizable elements of Georgia Tech (Tech Tower, honeycomb pattern, technology focus) while also expressing elements of mystery. The "chaos" attribute can be chalked up to the black, white, and gold color scheme, but also to the uncertainty which science makes sense of, and engineering rids of. The four LED tubes illumniate the chaos, and represent the role institutions like Georgia Tech have discovering and applying knowledge. I selected Bocote hardwood for the exterior, with a black honeycomb mesh covering two opposite sides. The top has the Georgia Tech seal engraved on it, and slides on and off with relative ease. A golden brass covered plastic block was laser engraved with Tech Tower and a short message in binary in ASCII, stating something to the effect of "Georgia Tech Chaos Clock", my name, and the date. Four ansonia clock feet were spray painted black and attached for a more regal feel. The bocote box was primed and lacquered.
