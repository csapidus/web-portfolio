Reverse-Polish-Notation (RPN) Nixie Calculator

Turns out there is more to nixie tubes than clocks! In an effort to become acquainted with microcontrollers, utilize nixies differently, and apply the RPN concept introduced to me by good friend Reid Graves, I elected to develop (without a kit) an RPN nixie calculator. Many headaches and one shorted Arduino later, it works! The wiring was messier than expected. Uses six IN-12 nixie tubes and an I2C LCD for a double display. Controlled with an Arduino Mega (knockoff) assisted by six K155iD1 nixie drivers. Includes a calculator-display specific matrix keypad. The plan over the coming couple months is to develop a custom PCB and encase in Zircote wood, while replacing the current keypad with Cherry MX Red key switches and caps. Also hoping to expand the display size from six to eight nixies, and add new operations (log(), e, etc.).

Estimated Building Cost: $110.00
